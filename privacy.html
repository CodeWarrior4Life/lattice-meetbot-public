Privacy Policy — Cody Zoom Meetbot

Effective Date: 2026-05-15 Last Updated: 2026-05-15

Cody Zoom Meetbot ("Cody," "the App," "we," "us," or "our") is a Zoom Marketplace application operated by Obsidian Inc. ("Obsidian," "we," "us") that joins Zoom meetings as an AI assistant to provide real-time transcription, conversational Q&A, action-item extraction, and post-meeting summaries. This Privacy Policy explains what information we collect when you authorize and use Cody, how we use it, who we share it with, how long we keep it, and the choices you have.

By authorizing Cody on your Zoom account or any account on which you are an admin, you agree to the practices described in this Privacy Policy. If you do not agree, do not authorize the App.

This Privacy Policy is published at https://codewarrior4life.github.io/lattice-meetbot-public/privacy.html and is referenced from the Cody Zoom Meetbot Marketplace listing per Zoom's Marketplace requirements.

2. Information we collect

Cody collects only what is needed to deliver its meeting-assistance features. Specifically:

2.1 OAuth tokens and account identifiers

When you authorize Cody on a Zoom account, Zoom provides us with:

  • An OAuth access token and refresh token scoped to the permissions you grant
  • Your Zoom user ID
  • Your Zoom account ID
  • The email address associated with your Zoom account
  • A Zoom Access Key (ZAK) token, when required, to authorize Cody to join meetings on your behalf

These tokens are credentials. They are stored encrypted at rest and used only to invoke the Zoom APIs and SDK calls necessary to join meetings, fetch metadata, and send chat messages on your behalf.

2.2 Meeting metadata

For each meeting Cody joins on your behalf, we may temporarily process:

  • Meeting ID and join URL
  • Meeting topic (where provided)
  • Scheduled start/end times
  • Host and participant display names and Zoom user IDs
  • Participant join/leave events

2.3 Meeting content

Once Cody is admitted to a meeting, we process the meeting content needed to deliver our features:

  • Audio: raw PCM audio frames delivered by the Zoom Meeting SDK while Cody is admitted to the meeting. These frames are buffered in memory, written to a temporary WAV file for the duration of the meeting, and (optionally) streamed to a cloud Speech-to-Text provider for real-time transcription.
  • Chat messages: chat messages sent in the meeting that Cody is admitted to. Messages addressed to Cody (e.g., starting with @cody) trigger LLM responses; other messages are buffered to provide conversational context to the LLM but are not retained beyond the session unless you opt in.
  • Transcripts: text transcripts generated from the audio, segmented by speaker turn when speaker diarization is available.
  • Generated content: AI-generated summaries, action items, and chat replies produced by Cody.

2.4 Information we do NOT collect

Cody does not collect:

  • Video frames (we do not subscribe to video tracks)
  • Screen-share content
  • Camera, microphone, or file access on participants' devices
  • Calendar contents, email, or any data outside the scope of the Zoom OAuth grant
  • Children's data (Cody is not directed to children under 13; do not authorize Cody if you are under 13)
  • Sensitive personal data such as health, financial, racial, religious, biometric, or precise location data

3. How we use information

We use the information described in Section 2 only for the following purposes:

  1. Joining meetings on your behalf. OAuth tokens and ZAK are used to call Zoom's Meeting SDK and join meetings you (or your account admin) authorize.
  2. Operating the in-meeting assistant. Audio is processed to generate transcripts; chat messages prefixed @cody are sent to our LLM provider (Anthropic) to generate a reply; the reply is posted to the Zoom chat.
  3. Generating post-meeting artifacts. When the meeting ends or Cody leaves, the transcript and chat history are used to generate a summary and action-item list.
  4. Service operation. Diagnostic logs (with audio and message contents redacted) are used to debug failures and improve reliability.
  5. Security. We may use access logs to detect abuse and abuse-of-tokens.

We do not use meeting content to train AI models. Meeting content is sent to Anthropic's Claude API only for inference and is subject to Anthropic's commercial terms, which prohibit training on customer inputs by default.

6. Retention

  • OAuth tokens are retained for as long as your authorization is active. When you revoke the authorization in Zoom, refresh tokens are deleted within 30 days.
  • Meeting audio (WAV files), transcripts, chat history, and summaries are retained for 30 days by default, after which they are permanently deleted. You may request deletion at any time before that.
  • Diagnostic logs (with content redacted) are retained for 90 days for operational debugging, then deleted.
  • Account-level identifiers (email, user ID) are retained while your authorization is active, and deleted within 30 days of revocation.

If you authorize Cody for a specific meeting only (one-off use), all derived artifacts are deleted within 7 days of meeting end unless you explicitly retain them.

7. Security

We implement reasonable technical and organizational measures to protect your information, including:

  • Encryption in transit: all traffic to Zoom, Anthropic, Deepgram, and GitHub uses TLS 1.2 or higher (HTTPS / WSS).
  • Encryption at rest: stored OAuth tokens and refresh tokens are encrypted with AES-256; persisted meeting artifacts are stored on encrypted filesystems.
  • Access control: each meeting is processed in an ephemeral container (one container per meeting); only the authorizing user's data is accessible within that container.
  • Least privilege: Cody requests only the OAuth scopes strictly required for its features.
  • Secure development: code is open-source, reviewed before release; secrets are managed via environment variables and never committed to source control.

No system is completely secure. If we discover a security breach affecting your data, we will notify you per applicable law (in the US, within 30 days where required by state law; in the EEA/UK, within 72 hours per GDPR Art. 33).

You are responsible for complying with applicable recording laws in your jurisdiction, including obtaining the consent of meeting participants where required (e.g., two-party-consent states in the US such as California, Washington, Florida, Maryland, Massachusetts, Montana, New Hampshire, Pennsylvania).
